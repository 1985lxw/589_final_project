The test scores are widley spread which suggests the model is sensitive to the specific training data 
used in each fold, leading to a poor local optimum. Especially in smaller datasets, the presence/absence
of a few instances in a training fold can have a significant impact on the tree structure and thus the performance. 
The training scores are more concentrated around 0.87, while some test folds outperform the training performance.
This suggests that the model is stable during training, but is not consistent on generalizing depending on the test fold.
